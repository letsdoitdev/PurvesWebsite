The Problem

Chronic Disease Is Bankrupting America

The deficit isn't a mystery. We are spending ourselves into ruin treating diseases that are largely preventable. Heal the country, and you heal the budget.

$2T
Annual federal deficit

Every year we spend two trillion dollars more than we take in.

$1T
Interest on the debt

A trillion dollars a year, just to pay interest on what we already owe.

The federal government now spends more on interest on the debt than on national defense. The cost of a sick nation is no longer abstract. It is crowding out everything else.

The CDC says 90% of medical spending is for chronic disease, including mental illness. Prevent the disease, and you cut the cost.

The Federal Reserve prints money to finance deficits, which is leading to hyperinflation.

Follow the Money

A System That Profits From Keeping Us Sick

This isn't about blaming one party. It's about an industry that makes money whether you get better or not. That's a problem the left and the right can fix together.

Big Food

Big Food is making us sick.

Big Med

Big Med has no financial incentive to cure us; rather it makes money by having us take pills and undergo medical procedures. When you tell the doctor you have high blood pressure, the doctor should say, "Let's find what's causing your high blood pressure." Instead, the doctor prescribes pills you must take for the rest of your life, which do not cure the disease and may have bad side effects.

The Solution

Left and Right Agree: The System Is Making Us Sick

Make America Healthy Again is common sense. Two simple ideas can lower health costs and shrink the deficit for Democrats, Republicans, and everyone in between.

Why We're Sick

It turns out that much chronic disease, including mental illness, is caused by unregulated artificial additives in our food and by the bad federal nutritional advice of the last 40 years: "Eat high carb, lean meat, and low [saturated] fat." That is a prescription for diabetes, which is now appearing in children.

Eat Real Food

What our grandparents ate. Simple, whole, and additive-free.

  • Full-fat dairy
  • Eggs
  • Red meat
  • Fruits & vegetables
  • Additive-free bread

Tax What Makes Us Sick

Put the cost where it belongs, on the products driving the epidemic.

  • Ultra-processed foods
  • Seed oils
  • Artificial additives

Make America Healthy Again!

Eat real food! That's how to slash the federal deficit without cutting a program!

About Arthur

Meet Arthur Purves

Retired computer programmer Age 77 No prescription medications

I'm a retired computer programmer with no medical credentials, except I'm 77, taking no prescriptions, and feel great.

"As president of the Fairfax County Taxpayers Alliance, I've spent 29 years studying how to cut taxes. Rather than cut programs, reduce demand through better policies."

Arthur Purves grew up in Washington D.C. and earned a BA, MS, and MBA from the University of Pennsylvania. He and his late wife, Carol, moved to Vienna in 1976. They have two children, who graduated from Fairfax County Public Schools in the mid-1990s. Arthur worked as a computer programmer for 40 years and is now retired. He belongs to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

For 29 years he has been president of the Fairfax County Taxpayers Alliance. He was treasurer for the Fairfax Committee of 100 for ten years, was a member of the 2014 Fairfax County Meals Tax Task Force, and in 2013 served on the Hunter Mill Citizen Budget Committee. He has served on three Fairfax County Public Schools advisory committees and is a member of the League of Women Voters and the Fairfax branch of the NAACP.

He was a Scoutmaster for his church, including for a Latino troop, and was elected to the Order of the Arrow. More recently he has served as an election officer, helped refugees with asylum applications, and has been teaching ESL along with a computer basics class for ESL students. He has a General Class amateur radio license, KQ4AXT.

Arthur has run in seven general elections: for school board (1995, 2003); for county chairman against Kate Hanley as an independent (1999); for delegate (2007); as the Republican candidate for county chairman against Sharon Bulova (2015); for state senate against Janet Howell (2019); and a third time for county chairman against Jeff McKay (2023). As Taxpayers Alliance president, Arthur has testified at most county and school proposed budget hearings since 1997.

"I practice what I preach: I eat real food and feel great."
